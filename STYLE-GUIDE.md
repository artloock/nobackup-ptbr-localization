# PT-BR Style Guide – No Backup

## Project Context

**No Backup** is a tactical horror game with FPS/bodycam presentation, mission-based progression, menu-heavy UI, radio communication, and environmental tension.

The PT-BR localization was developed to preserve the game’s atmosphere while making the text sound natural, clear, and believable for Brazilian players.

## Core Localization Goals

The localization followed these priorities:

- clarity first
- natural Brazilian Portuguese
- consistency across UI, mission text, and dialogue
- tactical tone without sounding artificial
- contextual adaptation when literal translation sounded unnatural
- preservation of original meaning

## Tone Guidelines

### UI and Menu Text
UI text should be:

- short
- direct
- readable at a glance
- function-oriented

Examples of preferred characteristics:

- action verbs for controls and interactions
- concise labels
- terminology that players can understand quickly

### Mission and Objective Text
Mission text should feel:

- operational
- clear
- goal-oriented
- slightly tactical, but not exaggerated

The wording should sound like gameplay instructions, not bureaucratic military documentation.

### Dialogue and Radio Communication
Dialogue should sound:

- natural in PT-BR
- tense when needed
- believable in a tactical horror context
- less literal, more spoken

Whenever direct translation created robotic or unnatural dialogue, adaptation was preferred.

## Style Decisions

### 1. Natural PT-BR over literal English structure
Literal translation was avoided when it sounded stiff or unnatural in Brazilian Portuguese.

Preferred:
- natural sentence flow
- context-aware phrasing
- spoken-sounding dialogue where appropriate

### 2. Tactical tone with moderation
The project used a light tactical/military tone, especially in:

- mission objectives
- warning messages
- operational dialogue
- institutional area names

However, the localization avoided exaggerated military jargon.

### 3. Neutral phrasing where English was ambiguous
English strings often used structures like:

- you
- we
- let’s

Since these could refer to solo gameplay, co-op, or radio communication, PT-BR phrasing was often adjusted to remain neutral when necessary.

Examples of neutralization strategies:

- using impersonal structures
- removing explicit pronouns
- rewriting the sentence to avoid forced singular/plural choices

### 4. Dialogue adaptation for Brazilian naturalness
Dialogue was allowed to be more natural and regionally believable when appropriate, especially in tense or emotional scenes.

This included:

- replacing overly literal expressions
- making reactions sound closer to natural PT-BR speech
- keeping impact without excessive slang or meme-like phrasing

### 5. Consistency across terminology
Repeated terms were kept consistent whenever possible, especially for:

- access-related items
- mission structures
- gameplay systems
- settings menu vocabulary
- collectibles and item categories

## UI Writing Rules

For UI text, the following principles were prioritized:

- use concise wording
- avoid unnecessary articles when the interface already provides context
- preserve strong readability
- avoid long phrases unless required by meaning
- keep commands and settings intuitive for Brazilian players

## Menu and Settings Guidelines

Technical settings were translated with accessibility in mind.

Rules applied:

- common abbreviations such as **FPS** and **ON/OFF** could remain in English when widely recognized
- more obscure technical terms were translated when needed for clarity
- wording should help non-specialist players understand what each setting does

## Dialogue Writing Rules

Dialogue was revised according to:

- speaker intent
- urgency
- emotional weight
- gameplay context
- solo/co-op ambiguity

Radio lines and mission briefings could sound more operational.  
Emotional or survivor dialogue could sound more personal and direct.

## Capitalization and Formatting

The localization aimed to preserve:

- original emphasis
- all-caps where used intentionally
- spacing patterns when required by the UI
- punctuation rhythm when relevant to tone

Intentional spacing in the source text was preserved to avoid layout and alignment issues.

## What Was Avoided

The PT-BR localization intentionally avoided:

- robotic literal translation
- excessive military jargon
- forced slang
- meme-heavy localization
- inconsistent treatment of repeated terms
- unnecessary English retention in places where PT-BR was clearer

## Summary

The PT-BR localization for **No Backup** was built around a simple principle:

**make the game sound natural, tense, and clear for Brazilian players without losing the original intent.**
